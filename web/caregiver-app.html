How Stroke Recovery Cards Work

What are Stroke Recovery Cards?

Stroke Recovery Cards help patients regain strength and independence through daily, personalized exercises. Each card is designed for patients with vision or cognitive challenges — large text, colorblind-safe colors, and clear step-by-step instructions. No medical training required.

🎯 The Four Exercise Categories

★ Hand Exercises (Blue)

Improve grip and finger dexterity with squeezes and pinches

● Shoulder Exercises (Orange)

Increase range of motion with arm lifts and circles

■ Arm Exercises (Green)

Strengthen arms and improve coordination

▲ Leg Exercises (Purple)

Build leg strength and mobility

🖨️ Output Formats

4 on 1 Page (Quadrant)

All four exercise categories on a single A4 sheet. Great for caregivers to reference multiple exercises at once.

Large Cards (2x Size)

One exercise per page, double size. Better for patients with low vision or cognitive limitations.

About: Design Principles

Stroke Recovery Cards are designed with accessibility at the core. Every visual decision prioritises readability for patients with impaired vision, cognitive fatigue, or colour vision deficiency.

Font Size Selection

Card titles use a minimum of 24 pt (1.5 rem) bold weight to ensure legibility at arm's length. Step instructions are set at 18 pt (1.125 rem) regular weight. The Large Card format doubles these sizes for patients with low vision. All text uses the system UI font stack (-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, Segoe UI, sans-serif) for maximum cross-platform clarity and consistent rendering.

Colour Selection Basis

The four category colours are taken from the Wong (2011) colorblind-safe palette, widely recommended for accessible data visualisation. Each colour is distinguishable under the three most common forms of colour vision deficiency (protanopia, deuteranopia, tritanopia). Geometric shapes (star, circle, square, triangle) provide a redundant, non-colour channel so categories remain identifiable even in greyscale print.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Laminate printed sheets for durability
  • Use whiteboard markers on laminated sheets to check off daily completions
  • Print in color for best readability
  • Keep sheets visible in the patient's exercise area
